Incheon, South Korea

Useful terminology
안녕하세요 annyeonghaseyo
Hi. This is often accompanied with a clasping hands and head bow gesture.
저는 팀 입니다 jeoneun tim imnida
My name is Tim. (Switch the 팀 for your own name). A word ending in a vowel sometimes has an 'ng' consonant added to it as letters can't stand alone in Korean and have be grouped in pairs or triplets. While it's often pronounced softly it can change where the stress falls when your name is pronounced.
나는 한국어를 못해요 naneun hangug-eoleul moshaeyo
I don't speak Korean. This can often confuse the listener as you are, in fact, speaking Korean to announce this!
김치 kimchi
Pickled cabbage. This is definitely an aquired taste, but you will have plenty of opportunity as it is served as a side dish with each meal. You can see all the different types in the Kimchi museum.
